Kapow MVP

A social gaming platform catering to casual gamers on mobile devices.

Elevator pitch: A Netflix for games.

For gamers, Kapow aimed to be the one place to discover and play games. For developers, it provided a platform with an engaged audience and easy-to-integrate APIs for complex features.

Target Audience

We studied market research reports of the Indian as well as global mobile gaming industry and identified common behavioural patterns around us before settling on the below profiles as our main target audience.

High school and college students

Age Range: 15-23 years

  • How they discover games:
    Students, especially teens, have great networks amongst their peers for discovering the latest cool stuff. Other than this word-of-mouth channel, sources like Instagram ads, Youtube videos and tech blogs also enabled discovery.

  • Games they play:
    Arcade games, multiplayer games and graphics-intensive action games were the most popular categories. Puzzle and strategy games were also popular amongst a certain crowd.

  • Why they play the games they play:
    Competition. Beating peers highscores or challenging them to multiplayer matches was the dominant motivator in play here. In college campuses, groups of students were frequently found huddled together playing a common game.

Office-going commuters

Age Range: 23-40 years

  • How they discover games:
    Facebook ads and game requests, in-app ads and ads in games they already play influence this demographic. Other than these, word-of-mouth is also an important discovery channel.

  • Games they play:
    Arcade games and mini games are quite popular. Recently, nostalgic games designed specifically for the Indian audience have found a large, dedicated audience.

  • Why they play the games they play:
    Passively killing time. Hooked to the in-game feedback loops.

Designing the product

We studied market research reports of the Indian as well as global mobile gaming industry and also identified common behavioural patterns around us before settling on the below profiles as our main target audience.

Initial product decisions

  • Solving the classic chicken-egg problem.
    Gamers would not come onboard unless there were quality games on the platform; game studios would not develop for the platform unless they had a sizeable audience. To solve this, Kapow monetarily incentivized game studios to seed games on the platform.

  • Multiplayer games were the focus initially. 6-8 games from genres like strategy, word and arcade would be developed and based on the feedback we receive, further content decisions would be taken.

  • Indian audiences were the primary target group.

Information Architecture

Playing games with friends was the centre of focus for the MVP. Aspects like leaderboards, trophies, in-game currency etc. were either secondary or not part of the initial version.

Task Flows

Basic “tasks” on Kapow included starting a new game (either single-player or multi-player), returning to a game when it is your turn and post-game actions like viewing score, stats, leaderboards or doing a rematch.

Since the games worked on HTML5, an in-app web view was opened whenever a game was launched. This meant that switching between the native app interface and the browser-based game interface occurred frequently and had to be handled seamlessly.

Wireframes

As seen in the IA, the app was categorized into 3 basic sections with 'games' being the most important of those. To reflect that, I designated 'games' as the central tab and also planned to distinguish it visually from the other two by giving it more prominence.

Future Iterations

Since this was the minimum viable product (MVP), only crucial functionality was built in this iteration. In future iterations, we planned on adding many more features based on the response to this MVP. Some of the features we had on our roadmap were:

  • Leaderboards to foster competition amongst the user base.

  • In-game chat and taunts.

  • Singleplayer game titles

  • Daily and weekly tournaments

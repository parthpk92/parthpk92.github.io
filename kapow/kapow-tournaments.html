Kapow Tournaments

Bringing competition to the forefront of the Kapow gaming experience.

Tournaments was a feature we arrived at in iterations after seeing the positive effect that enabling competitiveness and rewarding great play had on the audience.

Why Tournaments?

After observating people compare their scores post a game - especially after a good play - we prioritized the leaderboards feature and I designed its interface. Along with the standard global leaderboard, I also included a country and a friends leaderboard in my design to ensure that everyone had something reachable within grasp. With this initial iteration, we wanted to see the effect that enabling competitiveness had on engagement and retention.

A few weeks after release, we went through the data and it validated our initial hypothesis that enabling competitiveness and rewarding good players by displaying their names on a leaderboard would increase engagement. Not only did the leaderboard increase engagement for the top players, it also motivated others to beat the high scores and in turn increased their engagement. The retention numbers for those who had viewed leaderboards also increased markedly.

To build on the success of the leaderboards iteration, we decided to create time-bound tournaments with rewards for the top players. The same principles of competition combined with recognition/rewards for great plays applied here as well along with the added attraction of a limited-time event.

Designing Tournaments

Tournaments was to be a major new addition to Kapow, with online marketing campaigns also promoting it vociferously. For this reason, I decided to tweak the IA to give it the desired prominence - tournaments had an entire tab to itself with the app now having a 4-tab primary navigation.

The tournaments were categorised in three:

  • Grand: Major competitions with significant prize money. These were rare and often themed to match with recent popular events.

  • Classic: The staple fare. 4-5 classic tournaments ran every week.

  • Mini: Shorter duration tournaments with limited players. The prize money was negligible but you had an easier shot at winning trophies to fill your cabinet.

The Impact

Tournaments were a big hit with the audience! Engagement and retention numbers improved and there were positive comments from the community on social media as well.

Certain themed grand tournaments especially garnered huge participation. eg. the football world cup predictor tournament saw more than 50k people participating with almost half playing daily.

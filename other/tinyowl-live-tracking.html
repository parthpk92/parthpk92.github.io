Order Tracking

Improving the post-order experience for TinyOwl - a food-delivery app.

For a food-delivery business, the post-order experience is a critical part of the experience. Based customer feedback, it was clear that drastic improvements were needed on this front.

To actively manage this experience, TinyOwl took a significant decision to move away from being just a marketplace, instead taking charge of the logistics aspect as well. This project marked the first step in this direction.

Existing Product

As things stood, TinyOwl was only a marketplace. Thus, once an order was placed, it was the restaurant’s responsiblity to fulfil it; TinyOwl’s team had very little insight into what was happening on the ground. This resulted in quite a few issues:

  • Incomplete or no order tracking
    The customer was only notified about the order confirmation and dispatch (and this too if the restaurant manager remembered to mark it on their app)

  • No active knowledge of order fulfilment
    After the promised delivery time was over, the customer was asked whether the order was delivered. TinyOwl had no way of knowing whether there were any issues with an order and actively resolving it.

Analysis of support queries and talking with users made it very clear that lack of post-order transparency was a major anxiety factor.

Designing the solution

As mentioned earlier, the most significant decision was taken at the business level when TinyOwl decided to take charge of logistics itself.

I worked on the incorporating this change into the customer-facing app. Below are some tweaks I did on the order tracking page…

  • Focus on the real-time status.
    Prominent live order tracking with a big, clear map view. The layout was also significantly de-cluttered.

  • Making customers aware about the new feature.
    The map interface was shown even before the live tracking began to educate users about the introduction of this feature.

Little details

Some nifty touches that I'm particularly proud of:

  1. Push notifications convey important, accurate info at a glance.

  2. Pins are designed so that focus is on the delivery boy. Difference in size & colour makes it easy to distinguish when the pins are overlapping.
